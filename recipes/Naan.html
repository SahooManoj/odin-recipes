Indian Naan

Description

Naan is a quintessential Indian flatbread known for its soft, chewy texture and slightly crispy exterior. Traditionally baked in a tandoor, or clay oven, naan develops a unique flavor profile that is both smoky and slightly charred, making it an irresistible accompaniment to a wide range of Indian dishes. Whether served plain or enhanced with toppings like garlic or butter, naan is a versatile bread that complements the flavors of rich curries and hearty stews.

Often enjoyed fresh out of the oven, naan is brushed with ghee or butter to add a layer of richness. Its ability to scoop up sauces and hold flavors makes it an integral part of the Indian dining experience, whether paired with spicy curries or enjoyed as a base for wraps and sandwiches.

Ingredients

  • ⅔ cup warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • ¼ cup ghee
  • 2 teaspoons kalonji (onion seed)

Steps

  1. Whisk warm water with yeast and sugar until yeast is dissolved. Cover and let stand in a warm place for 10 minutes.
  2. Sift flour and salt three times into a large bowl. Add yeast mixture, yogurt, and 1/2 of the ghee.
  3. Mix into a soft dough, then knead on a floured surface until dough is smooth and elastic, about 5 minutes.
  4. Place dough into a large greased bowl, cover, and let stand in a warm place for 1 1/2 hours or until doubled in volume.
  5. Punch down dough, then knead for 5 minutes. Divide dough into six equal pieces. Roll each piece out into 8-inch rounds.
  6. Cover an oven tray with foil and grease the foil. Brush dough rounds with remaining ghee and sprinkle with kalonji.
  7. Cook one at a time under the broiler until puffed and lightly browned, about 2 minutes per side.

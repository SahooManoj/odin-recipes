Aloo Gobi

Description

Gobi Aloo is a classic vegetarian dish in Indian cuisine, featuring a harmonious blend of cauliflower and potatoes. These vegetables are cooked together with a medley of spices including turmeric, cumin, and garam masala, which infuse the dish with deep, earthy flavors. The cauliflower’s slight bitterness and the potato’s mild sweetness create a perfect balance, resulting in a dish that is both comforting and flavorful.

This vibrant dish, often yellow from the turmeric, is a staple in many Indian households. It is typically served as a main course alongside roti, naan, or rice, making it a versatile option for lunch or dinner. The simplicity of Gobi Aloo, combined with its rich taste, ensures its place as a favorite in Indian cuisine, enjoyed by both vegetarians and non-vegetarians alike.

Ingredients

  • tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • ½ teaspoon ground turmeric
  • ½ teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon garam masala
  • salt to taste
  • 1 pound cauliflower
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh cilantro

Steps

  1. Heat the oil in a medium skillet over medium heat.
  2. Stir in the cumin seeds, garlic, and ginger paste.
  3. Cook about 1 minute until garlic is lightly browned.
  4. Add the potatoes. Season with turmeric, paprika, cumin, garam masala, and salt.
  5. Cover and continue cooking 5 to 7 minutes stirring occasionally.
  6. Mix the cauliflower and cilantro into the saucepan. Reduce heat to low and cover.
  7. Stirring occasionally, continue cooking 10 minutes, or until potatoes and cauliflower are tender.

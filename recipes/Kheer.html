Kheer

Description

Kheer is a beloved Indian dessert that embodies the essence of traditional sweet dishes. Made by simmering rice in a mixture of milk and sugar, this dish transforms into a creamy and luxurious pudding that is often flavored with cardamom for a warm, aromatic finish. The subtle sweetness of the milk pairs beautifully with the fragrant spices, creating a rich and comforting dessert that is both indulgent and satisfying.

Kheer is commonly garnished with a variety of nuts such as almonds and pistachios, which add a delightful crunch to its smooth texture. Raisins are also sometimes mixed in for an added layer of sweetness. This versatile dessert is a staple at festive occasions and celebrations in India, offering a taste of warmth and tradition in every bite.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 2 cups milk
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • ½ cup basmati rice
  • ¼ cup raisins
  • ½ teaspoon ground cardamom
  • ½ teaspoon rose water (Optional)
  • ¼ cup sliced almonds, toasted
  • ¼ cup chopped pistachio nuts

Steps

  1. Bring coconut milk, milk, and sugar to a boil in a large saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Add rice, reduce the heat to low, and simmer until mixture thickens and rice is tender, about 20 minutes.
  3. Stir in raisins, cardamom, and rose water; cook for a few more minutes.
  4. Ladle into serving bowls and garnish with almonds and pistachios.
